Sikhism: A Way of Life and Faith

Sikhism, or Sikhi, is a monotheistic faith founded in the 15th century by Guru Nanak Dev Ji in the Punjab region, which is now divided between India and Pakistan. It is one of the youngest major religions in the world, with teachings centered around equality, selfless service, and devotion to one God. Sikhism emphasizes the unity of all humanity and rejects discrimination based on caste, creed, or gender.

At the heart of Sikhism are the teachings of the ten Sikh Gurus, enshrined in the Guru Granth Sahib, the holy scripture and eternal Guru of the Sikhs. Sikhs are guided by three key principles:
